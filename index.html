SSL Renewal Required

Netlify automatically provisions SSL certificates via Let's Encrypt.

Why is this happening?

Your Netlify site's SSL certificate is set to expire on —. Netlify requires valid SSL certificates to serve your site over HTTPS. This is a standard security practice to protect your visitors' data.

What happens if I don't renew?

  • ⚠️ Security Warnings: Visitors will see “Your connection is not private” errors.
  • 📉 Loss of Trust: Users may leave your site due to security alerts.
  • 🔓 Data Vulnerability: Information sent to your site could be intercepted.

How to renew

  • 🔄 Trigger a new deployment: Pushing new code to your Netlify site often renews the certificate automatically.
  • 📋 Netlify Dashboard: Go to your site settings > Domain management > HTTPS to manually check renewal status.
  • 👨‍💻 Contact your administrator: If you're not the site owner, reach out to your Netlify site admin for assistance.

Pro tip: Netlify certificates are valid for 90 days. Automating deployments or enabling continuous deployment helps keep them renewed automatically.
